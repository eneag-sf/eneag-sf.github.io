How it started

A month ago, before attending #dreamforce, I applied to be a Trailbalzer 1:1 Mentor to help other Trailblazer onboard on the salesforce ecosystem so they can explore new career opportunities. My goal was to make new trailblazers feel welcome in the ecosystem, broaden their network, inspire them and also answer any question that they might have regarding the salesforce career.

Acceptance email & Eligibility criterias

After a couple of days, I got an Email confirming that I have been accepted as a Mentor and that I have been matched with a Mentee.

Being a 1:1 Mentor

As soon as I got the acceptance email, I reached out to my mentee so we could align on the first meeting. As my mentee was in (GMT-5) timezone we had to both agree on a timeline which would suit us best.

She is very motivated and also gaining a lot of experience in Salesforce by completing badges/superbadged weekly.

I support her in the following mentor guidance tasks:

  • support with professional goals
  • create a career plan, review CV, and preparation or mocks for job interviews
  • connect her with recruiters in her region
  • help her out when choosing trailhead modules which are relevant for job interviews
  • answer various questions regarding the career, etc

Tips

I would always suggest to connect your Mentees with their local Trailblazer Groups (Admin, Women in Tech, etc). They are very helpful to extend their network which might lead to them landing a job faster. It also of course increases their confidence. Bonus points if they are active and participate as a speaker, which would look great in the CV.
